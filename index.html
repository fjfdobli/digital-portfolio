Reflections

Questions & Answers

Who was I back then?

I was someone who focused more on myself and my own goals, without really thinking deeply about others. I cared about my responsibilities, but I did not always pay attention to how others felt. I usually interacted with people on a surface level and did not go deeper. Looking back, I can say I lacked awareness when it came to meaningful connections.

What left a mark on you?

I learned the most about truly seeing and valuing other people beyond surface-level interactions. It made me realize that every person has their own story that deserves respect and understanding. It left a mark because it challenged how I usually think and act toward others” it opened my eyes to the importance of empathy in everyday life.

A particular instance of transformation?

There was a class activity where we had to listen deeply to someone else's story without interrupting or judging. I realized it was actually difficult to fully focus on another person. As I listened, I began to understand their feelings more clearly. That moment helped me become more patient and more open-minded. Since then, I have been trying to listen better and be more present.

Who are you most thankful for?

I am most thankful for my classmates and teachers who showed kindness and openness in simple ways. They made me feel that small actions can have a big impact on others. I appreciate how they listened, supported, and respected different perspectives. I plan to be more intentional in how I treat people and build relationships going forward.

Module 02 / Walking with St. Ignatius

My Conversion
Story.

A personal narrative of growth, struggle, and transformation through my years at Ateneo de Davao University” guided by the spirit of St. Ignatius of Loyola.

The Beginning Entering AdDU

When I first entered Ateneo de Davao University, I felt excited but also lost and intimidated. I expected that I would easily find friends, but I struggled because of social differences and ended up feeling isolated. Academically, it was also difficult. I failed in my first semester, and that became my wake-up call” similar to how Ignatius of Loyola was forced to stop and reflect after his injury in Pamplona. That moment made me realize that something needed to change in me.

The Shift Finding Community

In the summer of my first year, I shifted from BS Computer Science to BS Information Technology. The transition was not easy, especially since I only started my IT major subjects in the first semester of my second year. During this time, I slowly found the right people. As an irregular student, I built connections, and we supported each other academically. I began to experience cura personalis

The Turning Point Third Year

My turning point came in third year during a defense subject, where I carried most of the work because a groupmate did not help. That experience pushed me to become more independent. I started coding and building projects on my own, and I took full responsibility for my learning. My mindset changed” I accepted that life is not always fair, but I can choose to grow from it. I practiced cura personalis by learning to care for myself, prioritize my growth, and help others who are also struggling.

Now Who I Have Become

Now, I am very different from who I was in first year. I am more focused, disciplined, and confident. My experiences shaped not only my skills but also my values. As a future IT professional, I want to apply cura personalis by creating systems that not only solve problems but also help people in meaningful ways. My journey taught me that real growth comes from struggle, reflection, and care for both myself and others.

Walking with St. Ignatius

Reflections

Entering Manresa with St. Ignatius

Yes, I have experienced moments of peace when I felt I was doing the right thing, and moments of emptiness when I felt lost. I learned that consolations are those times when I feel calm and guided, while desolations are when I feel distant and unmotivated. Over time, I became more aware of what truly brings me closer to purpose and peace.

Sitting at the River Cardoner

My takeaway is that God's love is always present even before I realize it. Hearing "I loved you first" makes me feel valued and accepted despite my flaws. It reminds me that I am not alone in my journey, and gives me peace. In response, I want to live more intentionally and reflect that love in how I treat others.

Preparing Your Heart and Mind

The readings reminded me to slow down and become more aware of my inner thoughts and feelings. Hearing "I loved you first" makes me feel grateful and humbled” love is not something I earn, but something already given. This pushes me to become more sincere in my actions and decisions, more open, faithful, and mindful.

Encountering Friendship with Mission

After learning about Peter Favre and the early Jesuits, I see that friendship is not just about connection but also about growth and purpose. True friendship can guide and support others. In my life, I can use friendship to encourage and uplift people around me” friendship becomes a mission when it helps others grow.

Finding God in Action

As a student, I see God in the discipline and effort I put into my responsibilities. In my relationships, I see Him through acts of kindness, patience, and understanding. Even in small tasks, I feel a sense of purpose when I do things with sincerity. I also notice His presence when I overcome challenges and continue moving forward. These moments remind me that God is present in both the simple and difficult parts of my life.

Module 03 / Becoming an Ignatian Leader for Life

My Passion
Plan.

A revised Passion Plan centered on vocation, faith, discipline, and the kind of Ignatian leader I hope to become through technology and service.

Personal Mission Statement

Who I Am Becoming

I am becoming an Ignatian leader who uses technology with competence, conscience, and compassion. I want to grow into a disciplined and faithful man who keeps learning, leads with humility, and creates systems that solve real problems for people. Guided by integrity, perseverance, service, and cura personalis, I hope to build work that is excellent, ethical, and deeply human. In the years ahead, I want my success to be measured not only by what I achieve, but by the kind of person I become and the lives I help improve through my work.

Prayer for My Future Self

Prayer for the Years Ahead

Lord, guide the person I am becoming. Form in me a heart that listens before it speaks, serves before it seeks recognition, and chooses what is right even when it is difficult. Bless my studies, future work, relationships, and decisions so that they may reflect Your wisdom and love. When I succeed, keep me humble. When I fail, keep me faithful. When I am uncertain, remind me that Your grace goes before me. Give me courage, discipline, compassion, and hope so that my life may honor You and become a source of help, peace, and encouragement to others.

I want my future to be rooted in both excellence and service: to keep striving for more, not for prestige alone, but for the greater good I can offer through the work I do and the life I live.

10-20 Year Passion Plan

Long-Term Roadmap

Over the next 10-20 years, I see my vocation at the intersection of technology, leadership, and service. I want to become a professional who builds useful digital solutions, grows into trustworthy leadership, and remains grounded in faith, integrity, and responsibility.

Years 1-3

Finish my degree well, deepen my skills in software development and problem-solving, and begin meaningful work in the tech field. I want to build strong habits of discipline, prayer, and lifelong learning while starting to support my family and future goals.

Years 4-10

Grow into a dependable developer and emerging leader who can design systems that answer real needs. I hope to gain financial stability, take on bigger responsibilities, and mentor others while learning how technology and business can work together for lasting impact.

Years 10-20

Lead or help build a mission-driven team, business, or initiative that creates practical and human-centered solutions. I want to live a stable, meaningful life, remain active in service to the community, and build a future marked by generosity, purpose, and integrity.
